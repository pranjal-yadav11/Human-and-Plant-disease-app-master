Overview: "Bacterial spot," sometimes known as "bacterial shot hole," is a commonly encountered disease, especially on older peach trees. It  is an important disease in peaches, nectarines, apricots, and plums 
Symptoms: 
* fruit spots, leaf spots, and twig cankers
* Fruit symptoms include pitting, cracking, gumming, and water-soaked tissue
* Reduced fruit size, and sunburn and cracking of fruit.
* Leaf symptoms are generally dark, small lesions, often clustered at the leaf tip. Leaf tissue around lesions can turn yellow.
Prevention: 
* Good tree vigor should be maintained by proper pruning, judicious application of fertilizer, and watering when necessary. Excess nitrogen may aggravate the disease. 
* Planting the trees should be at appropriate distances.
