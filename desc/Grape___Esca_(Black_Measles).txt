Overview: Esca is a grape disease of mature grapevines. It is a type of grapevine trunk disease.  Some fungi cause this disease. 
Symptoms: 
* Leaves can dry completely and drop prematurely. 
* On berries, small, round, dark spots each bordered by a brown-purple ring, may occur.
* Cross-sectional cuts through canes, spurs, cordons, or trunks can also be observed.
Prevention: 
* use sodium arsenite as a chemical tool, particularly to control esca.
* Sanitation methods (quality of plant material, disinfection in the nursery, application of hot water treatment) and protection of pruning wounds either by chemical or Trichoderma have been recommended to limit the spread of the diseases.
