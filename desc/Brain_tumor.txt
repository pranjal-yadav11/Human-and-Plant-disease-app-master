A brain tumor is a mass or growth of abnormal cells in your brain. Many different types of brain tumors exist. Some brain tumors are noncancerous , and some brain tumors are cancerous . Brain tumors can begin in the brain (primary brain tumors), or cancer can begin in other parts of the body and spread to your brain as secondary brain tumors.
Symptoms:
General signs and symptoms caused by brain tumors may include:
* New onset or change in pattern of headaches
* Headaches that gradually become more frequent and more severe
* Unexplained nausea or vomiting
* Vision problems, such as blurred vision, double vision or loss of peripheral vision
* Gradual loss of sensation or movement in an arm or a leg
* Difficulty with balance
Prevention: 
A brain tumor can not be prevented. You can reduce your risk of developing a brain tumor by avoiding environmental hazards such as smoking and excessive exposure to radiation. Contact your healthcare provider if you experience signs and symptoms of a brain tumor.
